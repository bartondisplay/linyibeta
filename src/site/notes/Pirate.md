> [!summary] Summary
> **Pirates** are an unfortunate, if persistent, feature of interstellar travel. Opportunistic raiders who prey on merchant vessels, remote settlements, and the occasional poorly-defended shipping lane. While **Compass makes every effort** to ensure our rutters reflect the safest and most efficient routes available, the existence of such individuals remains, regrettably, a reality our readers should be aware of. 
> 
> In the core systems, piracy is uncommon. The presence of established defense forces and the sheer volume of commercial traffic make it a largely impractical pursuit for anyone with a modicum of sense. Incidents do occur, of course, but the discerning traveler need not lose sleep over the matter. It is in the outer systems where things become rather less civilized. Weak governance, sparse traffic, and communities that have perhaps grown too comfortable outside the reach of proper authority create conditions in which such individuals tend to flourish. 
> 
> Compass advises caution when travelling through these regions, and recommends consulting the relevant system entries for local advisories before plotting your course. 
> 
> _Compass reminds readers that engaging with pirates — for any reason — is inadvisable and likely illegal in most jurisdictions._
